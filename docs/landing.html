Backlog, tickets, sprints, and delivery run through explicit operations with append-only history, real metrics, and human approval gates. Fast agents, without the pile of untracked TODOs.

Every ticket moves through one owning operation. A state value alone is never proof its transition happened.
Why

Rigor over vibes

AI agents ship code fast. Left ungoverned, they invent scope, hide regressions, and report "done" with no evidence. A-Team is the control plane that keeps them honest.

Explicit lifecycle

One owner per transition

backlog → ready → in_progress → review → done, each through its own operation, each with a matching append-only event.

Ready & Done

Contracts, not intentions

A ticket enters a sprint only when outcome, scope, acceptance, and verification are pinned down — and closes only with real evidence.

Measurement

Metrics from the record

Lead, cycle, review and blocked time; throughput, committed velocity, first-pass acceptance, carry-over. Derived, never hand-set.

Token accounting

Cost you can attribute

Provider-reported usage attributed by ticket, sprint, and purpose. Unavailable counts stay null — never invented or prorated.

Composes with gstack

Product team, meet the dev team

A-Team doesn't replace your planning stack — it executes it. Pair it with gstack and the roles split cleanly.

gstack · product team

Designs the product

CEO strategy, engineering and design reviews, discovery, QA, and shipping. It produces the plans, findings, and evidence.

A-Team · dev team

Delivers it

Turns those plans into committed, reviewed, delivered tickets under Scrum discipline — owning priority, acceptance, closure, and metrics.

A gstack report is evidence for a Scrum operation — never a Scrum state. A-Team runs standalone too; gstack is optional.
